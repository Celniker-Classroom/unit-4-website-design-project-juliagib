Norway, the land of the midnight sun

Norway is in Northern Europe and part of Scandinavia. Their main languages are Norwegian and English. Norway is most famous for its fjords, which are long narrow sea inlets with steep cliffs, which are carved by glaciers. Additionally, Norway is famous for its many mountains, forests, and lakes. It is generally cold and snowy. It was also shaped by ice ages which is why you can see the extremely dramatic landscapes today. In the summer, northern Norway does not have the sun set for many weeks, which is why it is known as the "midnight sun". In winter, though, those same areas have what is known as the "polar night," which means that the sun does not rise above the horizon and it stays dark for almost the entirety of the day. Norway is also one of the best places in the world to see the Aurora Borealis. It is an extremely beautiful phenomenon which happens because particles from the sun hit Earth's atmosphere and create glowing lights in the sky that can give off a green, pink, or purple look.

Going outside for mental health is extremely important. In fact, Norwegians have a concept called "friluftsliv," which means "open-air life." They are saying that it is important to spend time outside, no matter the season. Being outdoors is greatly believed to help with mental health by reducing stress, improving mood, and helping people feel calmer. Traveling can be a very exciting way to get people to go outside and enjoy nature. The cities in Norway are also designed in a way with easy access to parks, forests, and trails so people can enjoy the outside more often.
