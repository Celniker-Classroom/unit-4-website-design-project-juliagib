Greece

Greece, the cradle of Western Civilization

Greece is a country in Southeastern Europe made up of a very mountainous mainland and thousands of islands. It is often called the "cradle of Western Civilization" because ancient Greece gave the world democracy, philosophy, theater, and even the Olympic Games. Its capital is Athens and they speek Greek there. It is famous for its mediterranean climate where it is extremely hot and dry in the summers and wet in the winters. Greece is known for its beautiful beaches, islands, and clear blue water. Additionally, their mythology which includes gods like Zeus, Athena, Poseidon, and countless stories and fables have spread throughout the entire world. People alos love the mediterranean food in Greece like gyros, souvlaki, feta cheese, olives, and fresh seafood. Popular sports in Greece are soccer and they love having a very bright nightlife and activities.

Greece begain thousands of years ago as a collection of independent city-states like Athens, Sparta, and Corinth. They were scattered around the Mediterranean sea. They were all built on farming, trade, and sailing. They developed early ideas of democracy, law, and citizenship. Over time, however, Greek culture spread through countless stories, religion, and art. Later on, they became part of the Roman Empire where it picked up the culture and language of Rome. Now, it has helped shaped modern Europe and the Western world through its many cultural influence.
